Methodology and limitations

Data sources. Decennial census counts of persons under 18 (table D08) from IPUMS NHGIS for 1970, 1980, 1990, 2000, 2010, 2020. American Community Survey 5-year estimates (table B01001, sex-by-age) from the U.S. Census Bureau for endyears 2011 through 2023. Each year between 2011 and 2023 is a real ACS measurement, not an interpolation.

Geography. Tracts are rendered on 2010 census tract boundaries. NHGIS decennial values join by nominal tract code; ACS values join by exact GEOID. Between decade anchors before 2011, annual values are linearly interpolated.

Area. Study area is the five boroughs of New York City plus every county sharing a land or water border: Westchester and Nassau (NY); Bergen, Hudson, Union, and Middlesex (NJ). Land area is computed in EPSG:2263 (NY State Plane, Long Island) and tracts smaller than 0.005 sq mi are excluded.

Delta view. "Change from baseline" shows percentage change in density from the selected baseline year. Red shades = more kids per sq mi than the baseline; blue shades = fewer. White = roughly unchanged (plus or minus 10 percent).

Known limitations. (1) Nominal tract-code matching for NHGIS can flicker when tracts are redrawn between decades; true population-weighted normalization is a future enhancement. (2) ACS 5-year estimates have margins of error - tract-level values carry real uncertainty. (3) ACS endyears 2020-2023 use 2020 tract boundaries; where a 2020 tract GEOID differs from its 2010 counterpart, the 2010 tract holds its pre-2020 value and the delta reflects that limitation.
